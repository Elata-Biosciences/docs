title: "How Elata Works"
---

## TL;DR

- Builders launch app tokens through a deterministic on-chain flow.
- Launches use ELTA and a constant-product bonding curve.
- Fees are routed by explicit fee kinds, not hidden heuristics.
- Governance and risk controls live in timelocked config/governor contracts.

---

## Launch Lifecycle

1. Register app metadata and contributor split.
2. Launch token stack for the app ID.
3. Activate curve after delay.
4. Distribute on curve until graduation target or deadline.
5. Graduate to LP and lock liquidity.

---

## Fee Lifecycle

- Fees are accumulated by `FeeCollector`.
- Fees are routed by `FeeSwapper`.
- Launch fee routes to treasury.
- App-revenue fee kinds route to contributors + treasury.

---

## Why This Matters

- transparent launch and supply rules
- explicit fee routing policy
- clear on-chain ownership and contributor accounting
- no hidden upgradeable logic in core flows
