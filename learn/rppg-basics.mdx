## What Is rPPG?

**Remote photoplethysmography (rPPG)** is a technique for measuring heart rate — and related cardiovascular signals — from a standard camera, without any physical contact.

The name comes from **photoplethysmography (PPG)**, a well-established method for detecting blood volume changes using light. Traditional PPG uses a sensor pressed against the skin (like the sensor on a smartwatch). **Remote** PPG does the same thing using reflected light from the face, captured by a webcam.

---

## How Does It Work?

When your heart beats, blood is pumped through your vessels in pulses. This causes **tiny, periodic changes in the color of your skin** — particularly in areas with dense capillary networks like the forehead and cheeks.

These color changes are imperceptible to the human eye but detectable with a camera:

```
Heart pumps blood
    │
    ▼
Skin absorbs slightly more light when blood volume peaks
    │
    ▼
Camera captures subtle color variation (~1% brightness change)
    │
    ▼
Signal processing extracts the periodic pulse from video frames
    │
    ▼
Heart rate (BPM) and heart rate variability (HRV) are computed
```

The Elata rPPG SDK (`@elata-biosciences/rppg-web`) runs this full pipeline in the browser using WebAssembly, with MediaPipe for face detection and landmark tracking.

## What Signals Can Be Extracted?

| Signal | Description | Common Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Heart Rate (BPM)** | Beats per minute, averaged over a window | Stress detection, exertion monitoring |
| **Heart Rate Variability (HRV)** | Variation in time between beats | Autonomic nervous system state, recovery |
| **Respiratory Rate** | Secondary signal derivable from HRV patterns | Breathing feedback |

<Note>
  HRV is considered one of the most sensitive biomarkers for stress, recovery, and autonomic balance. A high HRV generally indicates a well-recovered, resilient nervous system.
</Note>

## Requirements and Limitations

### What You Need

- Any standard webcam (720p or higher recommended)
- Adequate, consistent lighting (avoid backlighting or harsh shadows)
- The user's face partially visible in frame
- A modern browser with WebAssembly support (Chrome, Edge, Firefox)

### Limitations

| Limitation | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Motion** | Movement (nodding, talking) introduces noise; short-term motion causes signal dropout |
| **Lighting** | Flickering lights (fluorescent, LED with low PWM) create artifacts at 50/60 Hz |
| **Skin tone** | Signal-to-noise ratio varies; darker skin tones absorb less light, requiring careful calibration |
| **Distance** | Signal quality degrades beyond ~1 meter from camera |
| **Accuracy** | ±2–5 BPM typical for heart rate; HRV accuracy is lower than contact PPG |

The Elata rPPG SDK includes a **calibration step** to adjust for individual skin tone and environmental lighting before measurement begins.

## Combining EEG and rPPG

One of Elata's key advantages is that both EEG and rPPG SDKs are designed to run together in the same web application. This enables **multi-modal biofeedback** — combining brainwave data with cardiovascular data for richer signals:

- EEG alpha + low HRV → stress despite apparent calm
- EEG beta + high HRV → focused, resilient cognitive state
- Theta + dropping HRV → onset of drowsiness

See the [EEG BLE Integration guide](/sdk/guides/eeg-ble-integration) and [rPPG Camera guide](/sdk/guides/rppg-camera) for implementation details.
